What we are changing

The overhaul is moving toward a calmer, more usable layout that feels closer to a real product and less like a prototype with every idea turned up at once.

Summary Changelog Known issues
Layout

Natural structure

Reworking the page rhythm, spacing, and hierarchy so the app is easier to scan and feels less noisy.

  • Cleaner page sections and clearer page titles.
  • Less visual shouting from repeated chrome.
  • More consistent spacing across pages.
Usability

More obvious actions

Keeping the important controls visible and reducing the amount of guessing required to use the app.

  • Entry points for updates, reports, and feed navigation.
  • Better states for loading, empty, and error conditions.
  • Copy that tells people what a screen is for.
Visual system

Tighter styling rules

Replacing the scattered vibe-coded look with a more deliberate palette, spacing scale, and card system.

  • Fewer one-off styles and fewer random treatments.
  • Consistent pills, surfaces, and buttons.
  • Gentler gradients and more readable contrast.
Product direction

Current priorities

The current pass is focusing on the core experience first: the feed, profiles, posting, moderation, and the surrounding pages that frame those actions. The goal is to make the app feel coherent before any extra features get layered on top.

Changelog

Live notes on the overhaul work. Add new entries at the top as changes land.

Now

Creating a proper updates page

Giving the project a single place to record what is changing, what still feels rough, and what should be addressed next.

  • Dedicated changelog page added to the public site.
  • Links added from the landing page and app shell.
  • Written as a live note rather than a one-off announcement.
Visual pass

Cleaning up the interface language

The current redesign is trying to make the app look intentional instead of experimental.

  • Simplify the feed surface and make the reading order easier to follow.
  • Reduce the amount of competing neon treatment on controls and panels.
  • Keep the school-focused personality, but stop overusing it everywhere.
Product pass

Making the app easier to understand

Clarifying the main interactions so the site behaves like a product instead of a collection of demos.

  • Feed, profile, moderation, and reporting flows should all have obvious entry points.
  • Page labels and button text should explain where each action goes.
  • Empty and loading states need to say what is happening instead of just sitting there.
Next

Folding the rest of the overhaul into the same system

Any new features should fit the layout rules, spacing rules, and content structure established by this pass.

  • Normalize card and panel styling across other pages.
  • Keep new pages readable on mobile from the start.
  • Turn vague UI experiments into reusable patterns.

Known issues and rough edges

These are the things we already know about and expect to keep touching while the overhaul is in progress.

Needs attention

Some screens still feel like the old layout

Parts of the project will continue to look inconsistent until the rest of the pages are brought up to the same visual standard.

Needs attention

Features not working without signin

We are aware, that there are issues involving certain features, such as latest sorting for posts and catagories that are requiring sign-ins to work. This is almost certainly due to RLS policies, but isn't our highest priority at this moment. We currently want to focus on finding our identity and style.

Needs attention

Feature states may still be incomplete

Some interactions are present, but the error handling, empty states, or edge-case handling around them may still be unfinished.
